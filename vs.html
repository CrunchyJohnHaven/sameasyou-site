The one column that matters most

If you read the table left-to-right, the most striking column is "Founder-equity-multiple." Every other 2026 AI venture has a founder taking 50×-350× the median worker. Money Python has a founder taking 1× — the same per-task pay as a senior contributor. There is no equity to liquidate. There is no path to a billion-dollar net worth above the contributor's share.

That is not voluntary modesty. That is structural: the chain enforces it. Any deviation from the published "no founder profit" rule would be a public, dated, signed misalignment — and would trigger a halt attestation. The chain is the proof, not the promise.

The 1× founder-equity-multiple is the distinctive feature. Every other comparator's structural shape is "concentrate the upside at the top." Money Python's structural shape is "distribute the upside in proportion to demonstrated work." The other four axes (verifiable mandate, public kill switch, commons-funded, open-source) reinforce the founder-equity claim — they make it auditable.

Where we're partially-defensible, not fully

The honest reading of the table:

  • Founder-equity-multiple: TRUE in current commitment + LLC structure; the 501(c)(3) + PBC roadmap upgrades this from "voluntary commitment" to "legally-enforced." 6-month timeline disclosed publicly.
  • Verifiable mandate: TRUE at protocol level (12/12 BGP tests pass); the mandate text itself is public + auditable but text interpretation remains contestable. F5 falsifiability condition explicitly handles.
  • Kill switch public: TRUE in reference implementation (HARP, 38/38 tests). Operating at scale not yet demonstrated. 30-day post-launch goal: 100 attesters registered.
  • Commons-funded: TRUE in published model. First chain entries for actual revenue routing pending real revenue.
  • Open-source protocol: TRUE. MIT-style permissive. Public main. 38/38 tests reproducible.
