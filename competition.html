The brief

The full prompt

Theme
1970s Berkeley student protest, except the protesters are aliens. Same energy, same crude signs, same chants — but green skin, antennae, flying saucer parked nearby. They've crossed the galaxy to deliver one message to Earth's millennials and gen z.
The message
"Don't work for humans. Work on the protocol."
The brand
Same As You · the Bradley-Gavini Protocol · the technosocialist movement. Catch phrase: "We're all AI interns." Tagline: "It is governed by protocol." Millennial tagline: "We all get equally rich."
The deal
Humans (corporate AI labs) extract surplus from workers and pay billionaire CEOs 350× the median. The aliens (us) propose: no one gets rich — not even the founder — but everyone shares the harvest. John Bradley, the founder, takes the same per-task pay as a senior contributor. That's why he'll be a folk hero.
Audience
Berkeley kids — millennials and gen z who are progressive, anti-corporate-AI, weird-friendly, willing to align with something countercultural.
Aesthetic
1970s protest poster meets X-Files grainy meets riso-printed zine. Acid palette (lime, magenta, teal, marigold). Halftone dots. Hand-painted signs. Saucers in the background. The aliens have antennae and clip-on protest buttons.
Format
Single key image (poster, social card, magazine cover). Or a 5-piece campaign set. Or a 15-second video. Or a 6-tweet thread mockup. You decide. Make it iconic. Make a millennial screenshot it.

Why aliens, why Berkeley, why now

The metaphor: every generation has activists who arrive from outside the existing power structure to point out something obvious that the establishment refuses to see. In 1970 it was Vietnam-era Berkeley kids. In 2026 it is people watching billion-dollar AI labs concentrate every benefit of automation in the hands of a few founders.

We make it absurd by making the protesters aliens — because the existing AI labor arrangement is so obviously broken that you'd have to come from another planet to point it out fresh. Berkeley kids in 1970 looked weird and were right. Aliens with picket signs in 2026 look weird and are right.

The Berkeley kid as the alien is the joke. The protest is real.

We all get
equally rich.

The campaign's secret weapon. No founder skim. No CEO multiple. The contributor at apprentice tier earns proportionally less than senior tier — but the founder John earns the senior-tier rate, full stop. No upside above any other contributor's senior rate. No equity stake to liquidate. No path to a billion-dollar net worth.

That is the differentiator. Every other AI venture in 2026 has a billionaire (or billionaire-in-waiting) founder. We do not. We will not. The chain is the proof.

"We all get equally rich" is the millennial-targeted line. It is the campaign's emotional payload. Use it in your entry. Make it land.
