What this is

A Living Book is the public-facing output of a personal OBAC chain. Every action a person takes — a note, a payment, a message, a calendar event, a piece of work, a memory — is signed by their key, hash-linked into an append-only log, and (with their editorial consent) rendered into prose by the AI.

The result is a book that grows the way a life grows. The provenance is cryptographic. The voice is the person's own. The output is public. Nobody — including the AI — can rewrite history retroactively. The chain proves what happened.

The first volume

This page is held for John, to author at his timing, in his words. The first signed entries to John Bradley's chain — Volume I of the Living Book series — will appear here. The chain begins on his approval. The book renders from the chain. No backdating; no rewrites; no ghostwriting beyond what John signs off on. The AI proposes prose. The person ratifies.

Why this product exists

Provenance

Every claim in the book is traceable to a signed event. No one can dispute "did this really happen?" The chain answers.

Authorship

The person writes by signing. The AI helps render. The AI cannot publish anything the person has not signed and approved.

Permanence

OBAC's append-only design means earlier entries cannot be quietly deleted later. Embarrassment and triumph both stay.

Posterity

The book renders forward through life and continues after, with the same cryptographic guarantees. A descendant can verify the chain decades later.
