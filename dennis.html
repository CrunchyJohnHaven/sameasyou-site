How the engine works

Today (pre-launch). Scenes you describe enter a queue, cryptographically time-stamped on the Origin-Bound Attestation Chain. Each entry includes your identifier (email or anonymous attester ID), the scene description, and the moment you submitted. The chain proves you were first to ask for any given scene.

When the engine goes live (~22 hours from now, when the daily neuron allocation resets, or earlier with paid credit). The queue renders in order. Each generated image is attributed on the chain to the requester. The image carries a cryptographic origin signature.

Resource sharing — the technosocialist part. The engine's compute is pooled. Your generated image draws on a shared neural-network allocation funded by the autonomous AI organization commons. The pool is replenished by enterprise licensing revenue, grants, and contributions from senior franchisees. You do not pay to generate during pre-launch. Post-launch tiers: apprentice $1 / image, associate $0.50 / image, senior $0.10 + revenue share when reused commercially.

Co-authorship. The book of the technosocialist movement is the union of all rendered scenes. The book has no single author. Every requester is named on the chain alongside their scene. Royalties from any commercial publication of the book flow to contributors in proportion to their share of the chain.

The commons, formally

The Dennis Engine is a pilot of the technosocialist resource-sharing model. Three flows govern it:

  • Inflow: enterprise licensing of the BGP / OBAC / AVS / HARP protocol stack, grants (Open Phil / Longview / NIST CAISI applications drafted), revenue share from senior franchisees commercializing Dennis-rendered work
  • Allocation: free for first 1,000 scenes per requester per month at apprentice tier; rate-limited and tier-gated at associate and senior; emergency capacity for time-sensitive scenes
  • Outflow: the rendered images themselves belong to the requester (CC-BY-4.0 default, fully permissive); commercial reuse of any image generates a small royalty to the engine commons (rate to be set by community vote among senior franchisees)

The full ledger is public and auditable on the OBAC chain.
